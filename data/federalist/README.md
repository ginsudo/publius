
The parser is dependency-free: standard library only (`node:fs`, `node:crypto`, `node:url`, `node:path`).

## Data quality â€” corrections and open issues

See `data_quality_issues.md` (regenerated by the parser on every run).

The editorial standard is: do not silently overwrite the source. Any deviation between `federalist.json` and PG #1404 must either be applied as a logged correction with cited external sources, or left as-is and surfaced for editorial review. Two parallel mechanisms in `parse.ts` carry corrections, both with the same precondition discipline (the `from` value must match exactly one occurrence; mismatches throw):

- `SOURCE_FIXUPS` â€” raw-text replacements applied per-paper *before* block parsing. Use when the source text itself is structurally malformed in a way that breaks downstream parsing (a missing line break, a missing terminator). Each fixup is `{ paper, from, to, reason, sources[] }`.
- `CORRECTIONS` â€” corrections to parsed field values applied *after* extraction. Use when the source text parses cleanly but a parsed value (date, byline) is wrong. Each entry is keyed by paper number with per-field `{ from, to, reason, sources[] }`.

Both are auditable in one place and survive parser re-runs. An upstream fix to PG #1404 will fail the build rather than re-corrupt the data.

Current state: two source-text fixups applied (paper 11 PUBLIUS/footnote split, paper 37 missing PUBLIUS â€” both owner-verified with cited sources); one field correction applied (paper 26 dateline 1788 â†’ 1787, verified against Founders Online and three secondary sources); one acknowledged quirk (paper 24 footnote 1 missing period, owner-reviewed as cosmetic and parsed correctly anyway); zero open issues.

## Editorial decisions encoded in the parse

These reflect calls made during Phase 0; if they need to change, change the parser and regenerate.

1. **Salutation as first paragraph.** "To the People of the State of New York:" appears as `paragraphs[0]` on every paper. It is part of each paper as originally published; not stripped.
2. **`PUBLIUS` signature stripped.** The closing signature is metadata, not body text. Removed during parse.
3. **Footnotes structured in the `footnotes` field per the universal schema.** PG #1404 prints footnotes after the closing `PUBLIUS` signature, formatted as `N. Footnote textâ€¦` (or `EN. Editorial annotationâ€¦` for PG-transcriber notes on edition variants). The parser splits the body at `PUBLIUS`, extracts the trailing blocks into the universal `footnotes` field, and stores each marker as `(N)` / `(EN)` to match the inline reference form preserved in `paragraphs`. Three PG #1404 transcription quirks surface during this split: paper 11 (PUBLIUS line concatenated with footnote 1 citation), paper 37 (closing PUBLIUS missing entirely), and paper 24 (footnote 1 missing the period after its marker). Per the editorial standard, none are silently fixed: papers 11 and 37 are restored via owner-verified `SOURCE_FIXUPS` with cited sources; paper 24 is logged as an acknowledged cosmetic quirk that parses correctly. All three are documented in `data_quality_issues.md`.
4. **Title and dateline disambiguation.** A handful of papers (18, 39, 45, 58) merge the title with the dateline on one line in PG. Parser splits these by locating the dateline regex first; everything before is title, everything after is body.
5. **Disputed twelve attributed to Madison.** Following Mosteller and Wallace (1964). The PG #1404 byline for each of these twelve also reads "MADISON". The dispute is recorded in `authorship_note`, not erased.
6. **Joint papers (18, 19, 20) labeled "Hamilton and Madison" with status `joint`.** PG #1404 byline reads "MADISON, with HAMILTON" for all three; this preserves the joint character without elevating either name above the other.
