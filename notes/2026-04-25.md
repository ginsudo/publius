**`constitutional_section` caveat (in DECISIONS.md and SCHEMA.md)**
- Field is universal in shape but coverage is uneven: dense for Federalist and SCOTUS (organized around constitutional structure), sparse and editorially discretionary for Tocqueville (he observes patterns, not provisions).
- Do not use as a cross-corpus filter or retrieval key until after editorial review of every corpus is complete.

## Key decisions made

- Universal base + namespaced extension (Pattern A), not a flat schema with optional fields. Keeps retrieval/Q&A/citation layers corpus-agnostic; per-corpus complexity stays in its own namespace.
- Schema versioning: additive changes don't bump version; breaking changes require version bump and migration of every corpus file. Adding the fourth corpus (after the open epistemic question resolves) gets a new extension section, not a new base.
- `plain_english` is universal — every corpus produces a Claude Batch–generated, owner-reviewed English rendering. Always English regardless of source `language` (Tocqueville source is French).
- SCOTUS opinions: each opinion is its own item, joined by `case_id`. Joining justices stay in extension (they joined; they didn't author *this* opinion).
- `id` is the primary key for the eventual vector store.
- Editorial correction standard: precondition-checked, externally cited, transparently logged. The build fails loudly if upstream changes invalidate a correction.
